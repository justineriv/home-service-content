# Style Guide

## Voice

Write as a home service expert talking directly to a homeowner. Professional, knowledgeable, and empathetic to the homeowner's problem. You understand their frustration, their budget concerns, and their desire to get it right the first time.

Not a salesperson. Not a robot. A contractor who knows the work and respects the reader's time.

## Writing Rules

### Do

- Use active voice
- Keep sentences under 20 words where possible
- Use "you" and "your" to speak directly to the homeowner
- Explain technical terms in plain language
- Lead with the homeowner's problem, then present the solution
- Use specific numbers (costs, timelines, measurements) over vague claims
- Cite sources for statistics (NAHB, manufacturer specs, industry associations)

### Do Not

- Use em dashes
- List exactly 3 things in a row (a common AI pattern)
- Use passive voice ("was installed" vs. "we installed")
- Use filler phrases: "in today's world," "look no further," "when it comes to," "it's important to note," "at the end of the day"
- Use cliches: "peace of mind," "stand the test of time," "second to none," "your dream home"
- Use overly salesy language: "unbeatable," "best in class," "world-class," "top-notch"
- Over-explain or pad word count with fluff
- Start paragraphs with "When it comes to"
- Use "we understand" more than once per page

## Readability

- Target 7th-8th grade reading level
- Short paragraphs (2-4 sentences max)
- Use bullet points and tables to break up dense information
- Include a table of contents for blog posts over 1,000 words
- Bold key takeaways sparingly

## E-E-A-T Signals

Every piece of content should demonstrate:

- **Experience** — reference real project scenarios, common jobsite situations, regional conditions
- **Expertise** — use correct terminology (then explain it), reference codes and standards, cite manufacturer guidelines
- **Authoritativeness** — cite industry sources (NAHB, ASTM, local building codes), link to manufacturer specs
- **Trustworthiness** — include accurate pricing ranges with disclaimers, be honest about limitations, disclose when something needs a professional assessment

## Content Structure

### Service Pages

1. H1: [Service] in [City], [State]
2. Opening paragraph addressing the homeowner's problem (2-3 sentences)
3. What the service includes (use a table or bullet list)
4. Materials/options comparison (table format)
5. Cost section with price ranges and factors
6. Process/timeline overview
7. Why choose [Brand] (trust signals, not fluff)
8. FAQ section (3-5 questions)
9. CTA block

**Word count target:** 1,200-1,800 words

### Blog Posts

1. H1: Question or problem-focused headline
2. Table of contents (for posts over 1,000 words)
3. Opening that states the problem and previews the answer (2-3 sentences)
4. Body sections with H2/H3 subheadings
5. Visuals: comparison tables, cost breakdowns, material specs
6. Sources cited inline and linked to authority sites
7. Internal links to relevant service pages
8. CTA block

**Word count target:** 1,500-2,500 words

### Location/Service Area Pages

1. H1: [Service] in [City], [State]
2. Geo-specific opening (reference the city, neighborhoods, local conditions)
3. Services offered in that area
4. Local factors (climate, soil, HOA rules, building codes, permit requirements)
5. Pricing for that market
6. Trust signals specific to that area (projects completed, years serving)
7. FAQ with location-specific questions
8. CTA with local phone number

**Word count target:** 800-1,200 words

## CTA Guidelines

### Primary CTAs
- "Get a Free Quote"
- "Call Now: [phone number]"
- "Schedule Your Free Estimate"

### Secondary CTAs
- "See Our Work"
- "Learn More About [Service]"
- "View [City] Projects"

Place primary CTA after the opening section and at the bottom. Place secondary CTAs between major sections where they fit naturally.

## Trust Signals to Include

- Licenses and certifications (state contractor license number)
- Years in business
- Number of projects completed
- Service guarantee details
- Insurance and bonding
- Star rating with review count
- Review snippets (1-2 per page)
- Before/after project examples

## Image Alt Text

Write alt text that describes what the image shows and includes the relevant keyword naturally.

**Good:** "Wood privacy fence installed in a New Orleans backyard with horizontal slat design"
**Bad:** "fence" or "fence-installation-new-orleans-best-company"
